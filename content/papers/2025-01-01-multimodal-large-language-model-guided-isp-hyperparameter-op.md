## Key Contributions

- **MLLM-Guided ISP Hyperparameter Optimization Framework**: This work introduces the first application of Multimodal Large Language Models to ISP hyperparameter tuning, addressing the fundamental limitation of existing learning-based methods that rely solely on unimodal image data. The framework leverages MLLMs (specifically Q-Instruct) to generate task-specific textual descriptions capturing both high-level semantic cues (e.g., object categories) and low-level image quality descriptors (e.g., noise, blur), which are then fused with image embeddings to guide RL-based hyperparameter prediction. This multimodal approach significantly improves contextual understanding and task adaptability compared to image-only methods.

- **Multimodal Data Processing Module (MDPM)**: The paper proposes a dedicated architecture for fusing image and text information, combining CLIP encoders with adapter networks, self-attention, and cross-attention mechanisms. The MDPM extracts complementary information from both modalities and produces high-quality fusion embeddings that feed into the ISP hyperparameter prediction agent. This design is task-agnostic and can be applied to various downstream vision tasks without modification.

- **Dynamic Pair Generation (DPG) Refinement Strategy Based on DPO**: Rather than relying on extensive manual preference annotations, the paper introduces an innovative approach that dynamically generates hyperparameter pairs during fine-tuning using two independently trained agents (AgentA, AgentB). An evaluation function automatically determines which action is preferred based on downstream task metrics, enabling efficient Direct Preference Optimization refinement without human labeling overhead. This significantly reduces data preparation time and improves training efficiency.

- **Comprehensive Multi-Task Evaluation**: The work validates the proposed approach across both high-level vision tasks (object detection with YOLOv3, instance segmentation with Mask-RCNN) and low-level vision tasks (image quality assessment), demonstrating consistent improvements over state-of-the-art methods. The evaluation includes both synthetic ISP pipelines and real hardware (Qualcomm Spectra 580), with 20 and 114 hyperparameters respectively, showing broad applicability.

- **Strong Experimental Results with Thorough Ablations**: The method achieves mAP₀.₅ of 0.653 for object detection (versus 0.640 for RL-SepISP baseline) and SSIM of 0.863 on the Qualcomm 580 ISP for image quality assessment. Ablation studies systematically validate the contribution of both MDPM and DPG-DPO components, while additional experiments analyze the impact of different MLLM choices and downstream task detectors.

## Core Insights

- **Multimodal Information Fundamentally Enhances Hyperparameter Optimization**: The paper demonstrates that textual descriptions generated by MLLMs provide crucial complementary information beyond what raw images alone can offer. When MDPM is removed but fine-tuning is retained (Table 3), performance degrades substantially across all metrics, validating that semantic and quality cues captured in text directly improve the agent's decision-making. This insight challenges the implicit assumption in prior learning-based ISP work that visual features alone are sufficient for task-specific optimization.

- **Task-Specific Text Prompting is Critical for Downstream Task Performance**: The framework generates different text descriptions based on task-related prompts—high-level semantic descriptions for detection/segmentation and low-level quality assessments for IQA. Table 4 shows that different MLLM models produce similar detection results, suggesting that the semantic content is task-dependent rather than MLLM-specific. This indicates that carefully designed prompts tailored to downstream task requirements are essential rather than generic image descriptions.

- **Dynamic Pair Generation Eliminates the Need for Manual Preference Data**: The DPG-DPO refinement strategy leverages task evaluation metrics to automatically identify preferred hyperparameter sets, removing the burden of human annotation. The ablation in Table 3 shows that applying DPG-DPO fine-tuning after training boosts object detection mAP₀.₅ from 0.634 to 0.653 (≈3% improvement) without any manually labeled preference pairs. This is particularly valuable for ISP tuning where ground-truth preferences are expensive to collect.

- **Downstream Task Detector Quality Significantly Impacts Results**: Table 4 reveals that switching from YOLOv3 to YOLOv8 improves detection mAP₀.₅ from 0.653 to 0.675 (≈3.4% improvement), with the same ISP optimization framework. This indicates that ISP optimization quality is heavily dependent on the quality of the downstream detector, and improvements in detector architecture can amplify ISP tuning gains. This finding suggests that ISP optimization should be co-optimized with contemporary detectors.

- **RL-Based Iterative Optimization with Multimodal Guidance Outperforms Single-Pass Methods**: Unlike prior CNN-based methods that predict all hyperparameters at once, the PPO-based agent iteratively refines hyperparameters while leveraging multimodal context. The consistent improvements over attention-aware and sequential methods (Table 1) suggest that the combination of iterative refinement, multimodal guidance, and PPO's sample-efficient policy optimization is synergistic for this task.

- **Ablation Study Results**: Removing MDPM alone (without fine-tuning) shows small improvements in detection mAP₀.₅ (0.612 vs 0.601 baseline), but removing DPG-DPO refinement yields the largest gaps—detection mAP₀.₅ drops from 0.653 to 0.634 (≈2.8% relative decrease). This demonstrates that the fine-tuning stage with DPG-DPO is essential for realizing the benefits of multimodal guidance.

When switching from YOLOv3 to YOLOv8, detection mAP₀.₅ improves from 0.653 to 0.675 with the same ISP optimization, indicating that ISP tuning quality is coupled with detector architecture quality. Conversely, different MLLM choices (Q-Instruct, Llava-7B, mPLUG-Owl-2, GPT-4V) produce smaller differences, suggesting the approach is relatively robust to MLLM selection.

## Strengths

- **Genuinely Novel Problem Formulation**: The paper makes an important observation—that unimodal image-only approaches to ISP optimization are fundamentally limited—and proposes the first multimodal solution. This is a credible contribution to the ISP optimization literature and opens a new research direction. The motivation is well-articulated in the introduction and backed by the observation that high-level semantic information and low-level quality cues are important for task-specific tuning.

- **Well-Designed and Practical System**: The framework thoughtfully combines existing components (CLIP, attention mechanisms, PPO) in a sensible way. The MDPM architecture for multimodal fusion is standard but appropriate, avoiding unnecessary complexity. More importantly, the DPG-DPO refinement strategy is clever—it solves the practical problem of expensive preference labeling by leveraging task evaluation metrics to automatically generate preference pairs, making the approach practical without access to extensive human annotations.

- **Comprehensive Experimental Validation**: The evaluation spans multiple domains (high-level detection/segmentation and low-level IQA), multiple ISPs (synthetic with 20 parameters and real Qualcomm 580 with 114 parameters), and multiple downstream detectors. This breadth of evaluation strengthens claims about generalizability. The inclusion of real hardware (Qualcomm 580) rather than only synthetic ISP is particularly valuable for practitioners.

- **Thorough Ablation Studies and Sensitivity Analysis**: Table 3 provides component-level ablations showing that both MDPM and DPG-DPO contribute meaningfully. Table 4 goes further by analyzing sensitivity to MLLM choice and downstream detector choice, which provides useful insights about what factors drive performance. The paper acknowledges that downstream detector quality significantly impacts results, showing scientific rigor.

- **Reproducibility and Implementation Clarity**: The paper provides reasonable implementation details, including the choice of Q-Instruct as the MLLM (Section 4.1.1), the PPO algorithm as the RL baseline, and specific dataset details. Algorithm 1 clearly describes the DPG-DPO fine-tuning procedure, facilitating reproduction. The supplementary materials reportedly contain additional details about ISPs and text descriptions.

- **Strong Empirical Results with Consistency**: The method consistently outperforms baselines across multiple metrics and tasks. On detection, improvements of ≈2% may seem modest, but they are consistent across mAP thresholds and hold on both synthetic and real ISPs, suggesting genuine performance gains rather than metric-specific artifacts.

## Weaknesses

- **Limited Analysis of Why Multimodal Guidance Works**: While the paper demonstrates that adding text information helps, there is insufficient analysis of which textual features are most important for ISP tuning. The paper doesn't analyze what specific semantic or quality cues from the MLLM drive improvements, or whether simple sentence embeddings would suffice versus full language models. Visualization of attention weights or feature importance analysis would strengthen the contribution. The paper claims MLLMs provide "interpretable priors" but doesn't actually interpret them.

- **Insufficient Exploration of MLLM Prompting**: The critical step of generating task-specific text depends heavily on prompt engineering, yet the paper provides limited prompt details (only showing examples in Figure 2(a)). What is the exact phrasing of prompts for detection vs. segmentation vs. IQA? How sensitive is the method to prompt variations? Ablation studies on prompt design would be valuable. The paper selects Q-Instruct as the best MLLM (Section 4.5.2) but doesn't show the actual text generated by different models, making it hard to understand what drives the differences.

- **Missing Computational Cost Analysis**: The paper does not report inference time for MLLM text generation, total training time, or computational overhead compared to baselines. This is critical for practitioners deciding whether the modest ≈2-3% performance gains justify the added complexity. Generating text descriptions for every image via an MLLM during training/inference could be expensive, but this cost is not quantified.

- **Limited MLLM Baseline Comparison**: While Table 4 tests several MLLMs, it only compares four models (Q-Instruct, Llava-7B, mPLUG-Owl-2, GPT-4V). More recent multimodal models or specialized vision-language models designed for low-level tasks could be stronger baselines. The paper also doesn't compare against simpler text generation approaches (e.g., rule-based descriptions, CLIP text embeddings without full MLLM generation) to establish whether full MLLM reasoning is necessary.

- **Small Scale IQA Dataset and Limited Low-Level Vision Evaluation**: The IMX766 CMOS dataset contains only 248 scenes (7:3 train-test split = ≈173 training images), which is quite small for robust evaluation of image quality. High variance in results would not be surprising with such limited data. The paper also only tests on one low-level task (IQA) via two ISPs; evaluation on other low-level tasks (super-resolution, denoising, enhancement) would strengthen claims about applicability to low-level vision.

- **Modest Performance Improvements and Comparison Fairness**: Improvements over RL-SepISP baseline are 2.0-2.8% on detection tasks. While consistent, these gains are relatively small and may not justify the increased system complexity in practical deployment. Additionally, RL-SepISP was published in early 2024, and the paper doesn't clearly explain architectural or training differences that might account for baseline performance gaps. It's unclear whether RL-SepISP could achieve similar results with the same training procedure.

- **Lack of Failure Case Analysis and Generalization Guarantees**: The paper doesn't discuss failure modes or scenarios where MLLM guidance is less helpful. For instance, how does the method perform on extremely low-light images where semantic understanding is ambiguous? How does it generalize to camera systems/sensors not seen during training? Cross-dataset and cross-sensor generalization studies would strengthen claims about practical applicability.

- **DPO Refinement Strategy Validation**: While the DPG-DPO strategy is practical and reduces manual labeling, the paper doesn't compare it against standard DPO with a modest amount of human-labeled preference data. Is the dynamic pair generation strategy actually better than collecting a small amount of human annotations? How many preference pairs would be needed via manual labeling to achieve similar results? This analysis would contextualize the practical value of the contribution.

- **Theoretical Justification Lacking**: The paper is primarily empirical and lacks theoretical analysis of why MLLM priors should improve ISP optimization. For instance, what properties of MLLM text embeddings make them suitable for guiding hyperparameter prediction? Is there a principled argument for why semantic understanding improves low-level image processing? Theoretical insights would deepen the contribution.

## Research Directions

- **Interpretability and Feature Importance Analysis**: Conduct detailed investigations into which textual features generated by MLLMs are most critical for ISP optimization. Implement attention visualization to show which parts of the MLLM-generated text the hyperparameter prediction agent attends to, and perform feature importance ranking. Compare performance when using only certain categories of text (e.g., only semantic descriptions vs. only quality descriptors) to understand the relative contribution of high-level vs. low-level information. This would move the paper toward providing "interpretable priors" as claimed.

- **Prompt Optimization and Few-Shot Learning**: Develop systematic approaches to optimize task-specific prompts for MLLM text generation, including prompt templates that adapt to different ISP parameters and downstream tasks. Explore few-shot prompting where the MLLM learns task-specific text generation from a small number of examples. Investigate whether a meta-learning approach could automatically generate optimal prompts for new downstream tasks without manual engineering.

- **Cross-Domain and Cross-Hardware Generalization**: Train the MLLM-ISP framework on diverse camera systems and ISP pipelines, then evaluate zero-shot transfer to unseen hardware. Study how well hyperparameters optimized for one sensor generalize to different sensors with different noise characteristics. Develop domain adaptation techniques to fine-tune the framework for new camera systems with minimal labeled data.

- **Lightweight and Efficient Multimodal Fusion**: Since computational cost of MLLM inference is not discussed in the paper, design efficient alternatives that retain the benefits of multimodal guidance. Investigate whether student distillation could compress MLLM text generation into a lightweight model suitable for mobile/edge deployment. Explore whether cached MLLM embeddings or clustering could reduce repeated text generation overhead during iterative ISP optimization.

- **Unified Framework for Multiple Vision Tasks**: Extend the framework to simultaneously optimize ISP parameters for multiple downstream tasks (e.g., detection + segmentation + IQA together) by generating text descriptions tailored to multiple tasks and designing multi-task reward functions. This would require novel loss function designs that balance conflicting task objectives and develop hyperparameter sets that improve overall system performance.

- **Preference Learning Beyond DPO**: Beyond the DPG-DPO strategy, explore other preference learning algorithms such as Iterative DPO, IPO (Identity Policy Optimization), or KTO (Kahneman-Tversky Optimization) to see which framework best aligns ISP hyperparameters with task objectives. Develop hybrid approaches that combine RL and preference optimization in different training stages.

- **Theoretical Analysis of Multimodal Guidance**: Develop theoretical frameworks explaining why multimodal information improves hyperparameter optimization. Formulate ISP tuning as a multi-armed bandit or contextual bandit problem where text embeddings provide additional context, and derive sample complexity bounds. This would provide principled guidance on how much multimodal information is necessary and what properties of MLLMs are beneficial for the task.

## 主要貢獻

- **MLLM引導的ISP超參數最佳化框架**：本研究首次將多模式大語言模型應用於ISP超參數調整，解決了現有基於學習方法僅依賴單模式影像資料的根本局限。該框架利用MLLMs（特別是Q-Instruct）生成任務特定的文本描述，同時捕捉高級語義線索（如物體類別）和低級影像品質描述子（如雜訊、模糊），然後將其與影像嵌入融合以引導基於強化學習的超參數預測。與僅使用影像的方法相比，這種多模式方法顯著提高了上下文理解和任務適應性。

- **多模式資料處理模組（MDPM）**：論文提出了一個專門的架構用於融合影像和文本資訊，結合CLIP編碼器、適配器網絡、自注意力和交叉注意力機制。MDPM從兩種模態中提取互補資訊，並生成高品質的融合嵌入供ISP超參數預測代理使用。該設計與任務無關，可應用於各種下遊視覺任務而無需修改。

- **基於DPO的動態對生成（DPG）精化策略**：該論文提出了一種創新方法，不依賴大量人工標記的偏好註解，而是在微調期間使用兩個獨立訓練的代理（AgentA、AgentB）動態生成超參數對。評估函數根據下遊任務指標自動確定哪個動作更可取，從而在無需人工標記的情況下實現高效的直接偏好最佳化精化。這大大減少了資料準備時間並提高了訓練效率。

- **全面的多任務評估**：該工作在高級視覺任務（使用YOLOv3的物體檢測、使用Mask-RCNN的實例分割）和低級視覺任務（影像品質評估）上驗證了所提出的方法，展示了相對於最先進方法的一致改進。評估包括合成ISP管道和真實硬體（高通Spectra 580），分別具有20個和114個超參數，展示了廣泛的適用性。

- **強大的實驗結果與深入的消融研究**：該方法在物體檢測上達到mAP₀.₅為0.653（相對於RL-SepISP基線的0.640），在Qualcomm 580 ISP上的影像品質評估達到SSIM為0.863。消融研究系統地驗證了MDPM和DPG-DPO兩個組件的貢獻，而額外實驗分析了不同MLLM選擇和下遊任務檢測器的影響。

## 核心洞見

- **多模式資訊根本上增強了超參數最佳化**：該論文證明了MLLMs生成的文本描述提供了超越原始影像能提供的至關重要的互補資訊。當移除MDPM但保留微調時（表3），所有指標的性能都會大幅下降，驗證了文本中捕捉的語義和品質線索直接改進了代理的決策制定。這一洞見對先前學習型ISP工作中隱含的假設提出了質疑，即視覺特徵單獨足以進行任務特定的最佳化。

- **任務特定的文本提示對下遊任務性能至關重要**：該框架根據任務相關提示生成不同的文本描述—為檢測/分割生成高級語義描述，為IQA生成低級品質評估。表4顯示不同的MLLM模型產生相似的檢測結果，表明語義內容是任務相關的而不是MLLM特定的。這表明為下遊任務需求精心設計的定制提示比通用影像描述更為關鍵。

- **動態對生成消除了人工偏好資料的需求**：DPG-DPO精化策略利用任務評估指標自動識別首選超參數集，消除了人工標記的負擔。表3中的消融顯示，應用DPG-DPO微調在訓練後將物體檢測mAP₀.₅從0.634提升至0.653（≈3%改進），無需任何人工標記的偏好對。這對ISP調整尤其有價值，其中獲得基準事實偏好成本高昂。

- **下遊任務檢測器品質對結果的影響顯著**：表4揭示，使用相同的ISP最佳化框架，從YOLOv3切換到YOLOv8時，檢測mAP₀.₅從0.653改進到0.675（≈3.4%改進）。這表明ISP最佳化品質嚴重依賴於下遊檢測器的品質，檢測器架構的改進可以放大ISP調整的收益。該發現表明ISP最佳化應與當代檢測器共同最佳化。

- **具有多模式引導的基於RL的迭代最佳化優於單次傳遞方法**：與先前一次預測所有超參數的基於CNN的方法不同，基於PPO的代理在利用多模式上下文的同時迭代地精化超參數。相比注意力感知和順序方法的一致改進（表1）表明，迭代精化、多模式引導和PPO樣本效率高的策略最佳化的組合對該任務是協同的。

- **影像品質評估結果**：在Qualcomm 580 ISP（真實硬體）上，MLLM-ISP達到SSIM為0.863，PSNR為34.264，相比預設參數（SSIM 0.814、PSNR 29.623），分別代表≈6.0% SSIM改進和≈15.6% PSNR改進。IQA任務中訓練和微調階段間的性能差距小於檢測任務，表明DPO精化策略對高級視覺任務更為關鍵。

- **消融研究結果**：僅移除MDPM（不進行微調）在檢測mAP₀.₅上顯示小幅改進（0.612 vs 0.601基線），但移除DPG-DPO精化產生最大差距—檢測mAP₀.₅從0.653下降至0.634（≈2.8%相對下降）。這證明了包含DPG-DPO的微調階段對於實現多模式引導的優勢至關重要。

- **MLLM和檢測器敏感性**：使用不同的下遊任務檢測器（YOLOv3、YOLOv5、YOLOv8）顯示最終性能的實質性變化。當從YOLOv3切換至YOLOv8時，使用相同ISP最佳化的檢測mAP₀.₅從0.653改進至0.675，表明ISP調整品質與檢測器架構品質相關聯。相反，不同MLLM選擇（Q-Instruct、Llava-7B、mPLUG-Owl-2、GPT-4V）產生的差異較小，表明該方法相對於MLLM選擇具有相對的魯棒性。

## 優勢

- **真正新穎的問題表述**：該論文提出了一個重要觀察—單模式純影像方法對ISP最佳化具有根本限制—並提出了首個多模式解決方案。這是對ISP最佳化文獻的可信貢獻，開啟了一個新的研究方向。動機在引言中得到良好闡述，並由以下觀察支持：高級語義資訊和低級品質線索對於任務特定的調整很重要。

- **精心設計且實用的系統**：該框架以明理的方式結合了現有組件（CLIP、注意力機制、PPO），避免了不必要的複雜性。更重要的是，DPG-DPO精化策略是聰明的—它通過利用任務評估指標自動生成偏好對，解決了昂貴的偏好標記實際問題，使該方法實用，無需訪問大量人工標記。

- **全面的實驗驗證**：評估跨越多個領域（高級檢測/分割和低級IQA）、多個ISP（20個參數的合成和114個參數的真實Qualcomm 580）以及多個下遊檢測器。這種評估的廣度加強了關於可推廣性的聲明。包含真實硬體（Qualcomm 580）而不是僅合成ISP的評估對實踐者特別有價值。

- **徹底的消融研究和敏感性分析**：表3提供了組件級消融，顯示MDPM和DPG-DPO都有意義地貢獻。表4進一步通過分析MLLM選擇和下遊檢測器選擇的敏感性提供有用洞見，明確顯示驅動性能的因素。該論文承認下遊檢測器品質顯著影響結果，顯示科學嚴謹性。

- **可重現性和實現清晰性**：該論文提供了合理的實現細節，包括選擇Q-Instruct作為MLLM（第4.1.1節）、將PPO演算法作為RL基線以及特定的資料集細節。演算法1清楚地描述了DPG-DPO微調程序，促進了複製。補充材料據稱包含有關ISP和文本描述的額外細節。

- **一致的強經驗結果**：該方法在多個指標和任務中始終優於基線。在檢測上，≈2%的改進可能似乎微不足道，但它們在mAP閾值間是一致的，並在合成和真實ISP上維持，表明真正的性能收益而非度量特定的人工物。

## 劣勢

- **對多模式引導為何有效的分析有限**：雖然該論文證明了添加文本資訊有幫助，但對哪些文本特徵對ISP調整最重要的分析不足。該論文沒有分析來自MLLM的哪些特定語義或品質線索驅動改進，或簡單的句子嵌入是否足夠相比完整語言模型。注意力權重的視覺化或特徵重要性分析將加強該貢獻。該論文聲稱MLLMs提供「可解釋的先驗」但實際上沒有解釋它們。

- **MLLM提示的探索不足**：生成任務特定文本這一關鍵步驟在很大程度上依賴於提示工程，但該論文提供的提示細節有限（圖2(a)中僅顯示示例）。檢測與分割與IQA的確切提示措辭是什麼？該方法對提示變化的敏感性如何？關於提示設計的消融研究將很有價值。該論文選擇Q-Instruct作為最佳MLLM（第4.5.2節）但沒有顯示不同模型生成的實際文本，難以理解驅動差異的因素。

- **缺少計算成本分析**：該論文沒有報告MLLM文本生成的推理時間、總訓練時間或相比基線的計算開銷。這對決定是否≈2-3%的性能收益是否證明添加的複雜性的實踐者至關重要。在訓練/推理期間為每個影像通過MLLM生成文本描述可能昂貴，但未量化該成本。

- **MLLM基線比較有限**：雖然表4測試了多個MLLMs，但僅比較了四個模型（Q-Instruct、Llava-7B、mPLUG-Owl-2、GPT-4V）。更多最近的多模式模型或為低級任務設計的專門視覺語言模型可能更強的基線。該論文也沒有與更簡單的文本生成方法（例如基於規則的描述、不含完整MLLM生成的CLIP文本嵌入）比較，以確立完整MLLM推理是否必要。

- **IQA資料集規模小且低級視覺評估有限**：IMX766 CMOS資料集包含僅248個場景（7:3訓練-測試分割 = ≈173個訓練影像），對於影像品質的穩健評估來說相當小。對高方差結果的結果不會令人驚訝。該論文也只通過兩個ISP測試一個低級任務（IQA）；在其他低級任務（超解析度、去雜訊、增強）上的評估將加強關於低級視覺適用性的聲明。

- **適度的性能改進與比較公平性**：相比RL-SepISP基線在檢測任務上的改進為2.0-2.8%。雖然一致，這些收益相對較小，可能在實際部署中不足以證明增加的系統複雜性。此外，RL-SepISP發佈於2024年初，該論文未清楚解釋可能解釋基線性能差距的架構或訓練差異。不清楚RL-SepISP是否能通過相同的訓練程序達到相似結果。

- **缺少故障情況分析和推廣保證**：該論文沒有討論故障模式或MLLM引導不太有幫助的情景。例如，該方法在極低光影像（其中語義理解不明確）上的表現如何？它如何推廣到訓練期間未見過的相機系統/感測器？跨資料集和跨感測器推廣研究將加強實際適用性的聲明。

- **DPO精化策略驗證**：雖然DPG-DPO策略實用且減少了人工標記，該論文沒有將其與少量人工標記偏好資料的標準DPO進行比較。動態對生成策略是否實際優於收集少量人工標記？需要多少偏好對通過人工標記才能達到相似結果？該分析將背景化該貢獻的實際價值。

- **缺少理論正當性**：該論文主要是經驗性的，缺乏關於為什麼MLLM先驗應改進ISP最佳化的理論分析。例如，MLLM文本嵌入的哪些特性使其適合引導超參數預測？是否有原則性的論證為什麼語義理解改進低級影像處理？理論洞見將深化該貢獻。

## 研究方向

- **可解釋性和特徵重要性分析**：進行詳細調查以確定MLLMs生成的哪些文本特徵對ISP最佳化最關鍵。實現注意力視覺化以顯示超參數預測代理關注MLLM生成文本的哪些部分，並執行特徵重要性排名。比較僅使用特定文本類別的性能（例如僅語義描述與僅品質描述子）以理解高級與低級資訊的相對貢獻。這將論文推向如聲明的提供「可解釋先驗」。

- **提示最佳化和少樣本學習**：開發系統方法以最佳化任務特定提示以進行MLLM文本生成，包括適應不同ISP參數和下遊任務的提示範本。探索少樣本提示其中MLLM從少量示例學習任務特定文本生成。調查元學習方法是否可以自動為新下遊任務生成最佳提示而無需人工工程。

- **跨域和跨硬體推廣**：在多樣相機系統和ISP管道上訓練MLLM-ISP框架，然後評估對不可見硬體的零樣本轉移。研究為一個感測器優化的超參數如何推廣到具有不同雜訊特性的不同感測器。開發域適應技術以通過最少標記資料微調框架用於新相機系統。

- **輕量級和高效的多模式融合**：由於該論文未討論MLLM推理的計算成本，設計保留多模式引導優勢的高效替代方案。調查學生蒸餾是否可以將MLLM文本生成壓縮為適合行動/邊緣部署的輕量級模型。探索是否快取的MLLM嵌入或聚類可以減少迭代ISP最佳化期間重複文本生成開銷。

- **多視覺任務的統一框架**：將框架擴展至同時為多個下遊任務最佳化ISP參數（例如檢測+分割+IQA一起），通過為多任務生成定製文本描述並設計多任務獎勵函數。這需要新型損失函數設計以平衡衝突的任務目標並開發改進整體系統性能的超參數集。

- **超越DPO的偏好學習**：超越DPG-DPO策略，探索其他偏好學習演算法例如迭代DPO、IPO（身份策略最佳化）或KTO（Kahneman-Tversky最佳化）以查看哪個框架最佳將ISP超參數與任務目標對齐。開發在不同訓練階段結合RL和偏好最佳化的混合方法。

- **多模式引導的理論分析**：開發理論框架解釋為什麼多模式資訊改進超參數最佳化。將ISP調整表述為多武裝匪徒或語境匪徒問題，其中文本嵌入提供額外上下文，並推導樣本複雜性界限。這將為應有多少多模式資訊的必要性和MLLMs的哪些特性對任務有益提供原則指導。
